Frequently Asked Questions

How does the app work?

The operator has to load a master file, a scenario (if necessary) and a result file into the program and press the GO button. It automatically compares your data with the master file and displays the deviation as three indicators

What do the indicators mean?

1. Average, % - indicates the deviation from the pressure accuracy throughout the test
2. Detail, % - indicates the deviation from the accuracy of the pressure operation at the point with the maximum deviation
3. Difference, bar - indicates the average pressure deviation from the data in the master file
The higher the percentage of your deviation, the worse it is.
Normal deviation for a serviceable channel is in the range 0-20 %.When the values are in this range, they are highlighted in green.
A deviation in the range 20-30 % is considered borderline. The use of solenoids or valve bodies with these values causes risks. When the values are within this limit, they are highlighted in yellow.
Valve bodies and solenoids with a deviation of more than 30 % are invalid. When the values are within this limit, they are highlighted in red.
The software also gives a short commentary on the results. This is the for each pressure channel.
